Rendering of MathML math-030

The test passes if you are able to visually see the math equation (which should look similar to the reference image of the math equation). The test fails if you are unable to see the MathML-rendered equation or the rendering looks distorted. Note: The equation may look a little different (i.e. not pixel perfect), but the content of the MathML-rendered math equation should be equivalent to the content in the PNG reference image.

Navigating MathML with AT and other technologies math-040

The test passes if the following steps succeed.

  • The AT or other reading technologies report presence of all 4 MathML expressions and provide functionality to read it.
  • While navigating the mathML equation you are able to see the portions of the equation highlighted as you navigate the various parts of the equation.

If the test passes, please provide notes about your MathML reading experience, and how well you were able to navigate in MathML. For example, did you hear the prefix "mathml alt text” and you heard the expression "(mathml alt text): x sub 1, y sub 1" or was the math expression expressed via the readers own math rulesets such as "left parenthesis x subscript 1, y subscript 1 right parenthesis" with no prefix.
